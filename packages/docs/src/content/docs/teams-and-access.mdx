Vivd is built for projects that may involve internal staff and customer-side collaborators.

## Main access model

- Organizations own projects.
- Team members work across the organization according to their role.
- Client editors can be limited to a specific project and a narrower editing scope.

## Role model at a glance

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Agency lead or org admin">
    <ul>
      <li>Owns project setup, access decisions, plugins, and publish readiness.</li>
      <li>Best fit for domain, launch, and customer handoff decisions.</li>
    </ul>
  </Card>
  <Card title="Internal teammate or collaborator">
    <ul>
      <li>Helps with generation, Studio iteration, content review, and operational follow-up.</li>
      <li>Best fit for day-to-day production work inside the organization.</li>
    </ul>
  </Card>
  <Card title="Client editor">
    <ul>
      <li>Reviews project content and makes narrower edits where appropriate.</li>
      <li>Best fit for customer-side review without full organization control.</li>
    </ul>
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

## Recommended handoff flow

1. Keep project setup and launch preparation with the internal or agency team first.
2. Add customer-side collaborators only after the project, plugins, and publish direction are clear.
3. Confirm the correct organization and project before asking a client to review.
4. Re-check access again before launch so reviewers can see the right project but cannot change the wrong things.

## When to review access

- Before handing a project to a customer.
- When a project changes owner.
- Before launch, so the right people can review content and the wrong people cannot change it.

## Good practice

- Keep project responsibility clear.
- Grant the narrowest access that still lets someone do their job.
- Re-check access after project transfers or staffing changes.

<Aside type="caution" title="Before giving customer access">
  Confirm the intended organization, project, and publish path first. Most access confusion comes from people landing in the wrong workspace rather than from a broken permission model.
</Aside>
